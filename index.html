<a  style="float:right;width:200px;height:200px" href="http://www.omprakashsingh.com/"> Dr.O.P.Singh</a>

• Research Field: My field of research is double diffusion convection (DDC). DDC has fascinated many researchers over the years because of its wide application in various fields of oceanography, astrophysics, chemistry, geology, metallurgy and growth of semiconductor crystal. In oceans when the faster diffusing component (heat) stabilizes and slower diffusing component (salt) destabilizing the system with overall stratification remains stable, the convection takes the form of rising and sinking cells called salt fingers Double diffusive salt fingers plays an important role in transport process of ocean like vertical mixing and upwelling of the nutrients, which impact overturning circulation of the oceans, climatic control of the earth and support flora and fauna.My recent research findings include.
• A New Formulation for the Growth Rate of Double Diffusion Salt Fingers : New theoretical model for the growth rate of double diffusive salt finger is developed from the linearized governing equations with explicit dependence on governing parameters of the system such as Rayleigh number, density stability ratio, Prandtl number, Schmitt number and Lewis number. Results predicted by new-formulation are validated with the data reported from field management, experiments and numerical simulation.
• Assumptions in Linear stability theories of double diffusive finger convection : when are they invalid? The degree of compensation between temperature and salinity gradients in terms of their effects on density stratification is measured by a dimensionless quantity, density ratio (RÏ). Most of the double diffusive activities occur in oceans at low RÏ and most distinctive fingers form when RÏ approaches one. The theories proposed so far are based on linear stratification of temperature and salinity which were solved by many researchers under some assumptions. However, existing theories reported in literature unable to predict the behavior of the fingers when density ratio approaches one. I provide a new insight into the physical understanding of the assumptions taken by various theories and show the exact solution by discarding certain assumptions, which eliminates the limitations of the approximated solution and presented the exact solution of the cubic equation for the growth rate of double diffusive fingers and corresponding flux ratio, which gives bounded solution for RÏ approaches one and applied for a wider range of diffusivity ratios. New Results are validated from the data reported from experiments, numerical simulations and field.
• Effect of Rayleigh numbers on characteristics of double diffusive finger convection in variety of systems like stellar and planetary interiors, magma chambers, oceans and liquid metals : The new eigenvalue expression for the growth rate of double diffusive finger includes the effect of important governing parameter, Rayleigh number, which was not studied by previous linear theories. Growth rate and corresponding flux ratio, finger wavelength and other characteristics of the double of diffusive fingers are calculated by the new formulation which is computed for the wide range of Prandtl number (Pr =10^-4 to10^4) , diffusivity ratio (Ï„=1 to10^8) and Rayleigh number (RaT=10^3 to 10^12). The effectiveness of the model is shown not only for the oceanography and laboratory observed salt-sugar system but for the wide variety of fluid systems, ranging from stellar and planetary interiors to magma chambers and these results are validated from the data obtained from experiments, numerical simulation and field measurements. This model can be well applied in the study of double diffusion convection in astrophysics, geology, chemistry, metallurgy and oceanography.
• Linear stability analysis of the new model : The dimensionless numbers such as Rayleigh number, density stability ratio, Prandtl number, Schmitt number and Lewis number play an important role in the formation of double diffusive salt fingers, however, the dependency of finger instability limit on these parameters are yet to be known as linear stability analysis rely on single parameter. Further investigation of the new formulation could provide a valuable insight for the suitable condition of finger formation in variety of double diffusive systems.
• Investigate the effect of non-Newtonian fluid on convective structure and flux ratio of double diffusive fingers: Developing a FORTRAN code to numerically solve the partial differential equation governing the continuity of mass, momentum, energy and salinity in two-dimension and study the effect of non-Newtonian fluid on convective structure and flux ratio of double diffusive fingers.

• Brake cooling system using co-simulation , CD-Adapco (December 2013-Feburary 2014): To find the temperature distribution in brake disc system using co-simulation methodology in STAR-CCM+, a commercial GUI based CFD package. Determination of the temperature distribution and a peak temperature help in the improvement of aerodynamic conditions in and around the brake assembly, reduces the risk of thermal brake failure and hence increases the component life. Co-simulation is a special feature in STAR-CCM+ to couple two running simulation together. It is advantageous to use it when dealing with widely varying time scales, i.e. the timescale of thermal conduction in solid are much longer than fluids. Hence fluid is treated as a steady state simulation and solid as a transient simulation. Co-simulation combines these two varying time scale simulation and at one instance of STAR-CCM+ solves the solid domain and at the other solves the fluid domain, exchanging the data with each other. This technique saves CPU time and overall computational cost.
• Conjugate heat transfer analysis of a real life object, submitted in Applied CFD (ME-615) (Aug-Nov 2013): An object is clicked from various angles and 3-D model is created with the help of â€˜123D Catchâ€™.
